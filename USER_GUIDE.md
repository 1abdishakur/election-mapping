# Election Dashboard: Easy User Guide

## 1. What is this tool?
This dashboard is a simple way to see and understand election results as they come in. It takes raw numbers and displays them on a map and in clear charts so everyone can see who is leading and where people are voting.

---

## 2. Using the Interactive Map
The map is the main part of the screen. You can use it to see different results across the country.

*   **Move & Zoom:** Use your mouse or the (+) and (-) buttons to move around the map and look at specific areas.
*   **Hovering (Quick Info):** Move your mouse over an area to see a small box with the area’s name and its basic stats.
*   **Clicking (Full Details):** Click on an area to see its full results. The charts on the right will update, and you will see a list of the people who won seats in that area.
*   **Change the View:** Use the menu at the top-right to change how the map is colored:
    *   **Voter Turnout:** See which areas have the most people voting.
    *   **Winners:** See which party is currently leading in each area.
    *   **Close Races:** See which areas have very close results between the top parties.

---

## 3. Reading the Charts
The side panels provide more detailed information about the election.

*   **Left Side (Parties):** This is a list of all political parties. Click on a party name to see everywhere they are leading on the map.
*   **Right Side (Results):**
    *   **Vote Share:** Shows the percentage of total votes for each party.
    *   **Seat Share:** Shows how many seats each party has won so far.
    *   **Gender Split:** Shows how many men and women have been elected.
    *   **Council Layout:** A visual picture of the seats. Each dot represents one seat on the council.

---

## 4. How to Update Results
The dashboard is connected to a **Central Data Record**. When results are updated in the record, the dashboard updates automatically.

*   **How to update:** Simply type the newest results into your central data file.
*   **When will it show?** Once you save your changes, just refresh the dashboard page in your web browser. The new numbers will appear on the map and charts instantly.
*   **Important:** Do not delete or rename the names in the top row of your data file, or the dashboard will not know where to find the numbers.

---

## 5. Saving and Printing
*   **Export to PDF:** Click the "Export Map" button at the top to save your current view as a high-quality document. You can choose different paper sizes (like A4 or A3) to print or share.
